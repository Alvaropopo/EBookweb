2016
Now a Ministry of Magic employee, Harry Potter struggles with his past and his strained relationship with his middle son, Albus Severus Potter. Albus feels overshadowed by his father's legacy and forms an unexpected friendship with Scorpius Malfoy, the son of Draco Malfoy.

As the mission to protect the bridge builder Tazuna continues, Naruto, Sasuke, and Sakura are pushed to their limits—especially after Haku, Zabuza's loyal companion, reveals the full power of his deadly Crystal Ice Mirrors jutsu.
The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes
Arthur Conan Doyle
1892
This is a classic collection of twelve short stories featuring the brilliant detective Sherlock Holmes and his loyal companion Dr. John Watson. Set in Victorian London, the stories showcase Holmes’s legendary powers of observation and deduction as he solves mysteries that baffle even Scotland Yard.

Atomic Habits
James Clear
2018
Atomic Habits is a practical and insightful guide to building good habits and breaking bad ones through small, incremental changes. James Clear argues that success doesn’t come from huge transformations, but from the compound effect of tiny, consistent improvements—what he calls “atomic habits.” Drawing on psychology, neuroscience, and real-life examples, he introduces a framework known as the Four Laws of Behavior Change: make it obvious, make it attractive, make it easy, and make it satisfying.
Becoming
Michelle Obama
2018
Becoming is a deeply personal memoir by Michelle Obama, the former First Lady of the United States, in which she reflects on the experiences that shaped her identity, values, and worldview. The book is divided into three sections—"Becoming Me," "Becoming Us," and "Becoming More"—tracing her journey from a working-class upbringing on the South Side of Chicago to her time in the White House.
Caste the origins of our discontents
Isabel Wilkerson
2020
Caste is a powerful and thought-provoking examination of the unspoken social hierarchy that has shaped America. Pulitzer Prize-winning author Isabel Wilkerson argues that beyond race and class, the United States is governed by a rigid caste system—a deeply embedded structure that ranks people based on perceived human worth.
Educated
Tara Westover
2018
In this powerful memoir, Tara Westover recounts her journey from growing up in a strict, isolated Mormon survivalist family in rural Idaho—where she never attended school—to earning a PhD from Cambridge University. It's a story of resilience, self-invention, and the transformative power of education.
Quiet
Susan Cain
2012
Susan Cain challenges the cultural bias toward extroversion and celebrates the quiet strength of introverts. Drawing on research in psychology and neuroscience, as well as stories from real people, she illustrates how introverts thrive and contribute in work, education, and relationships.
Sapiens
Yuval Noah Harari
2011
This global bestseller traces the history of Homo sapiens from prehistoric times to the 21st century. Harari explores how cognitive, agricultural, and scientific revolutions have shaped human societies, belief systems, economies, and politics. With clarity and wit, he challenges conventional wisdom about what it means to be human and how we got here.
The body
Bill Bryson
2019
Bill Bryson takes readers on an extraordinary tour of the human body, combining scientific facts with his trademark humor. From skin to brain, he explains the mysteries of our anatomy and physiology, making complex biology accessible and entertaining for general readers.
The glass castle
Jeannette Walls
2005
This memoir tells the story of Jeannette Walls’ unconventional and often chaotic upbringing with eccentric, nomadic parents. Despite poverty and instability, Walls and her siblings found strength and independence, ultimately carving out successful lives. It's a tale of survival, love, and resilience.
